# Problem Statement

_boldtext_ Small  Business  Owners  Oftened  Struggled  With  Manually  Tracking  Their  Inventories.  This  Leads  to    Inaccuracies,  Such _boldtext_  As  Running   Out  of  Popular  Items  (Lost Sales)  Or  OverStocking  Perishable  Goods (Lost  Money).  They  Need  a _boldtext_  Simpled,  Reliabled  System  to  known  What  they  have  in  their  stock ,  what  they  need  to  Order,  and  which  Products  are  in  _boldtext_  the  System.

## Scope or existence of Project

_boldtext_ This  project  aims  to  provide  a  core-functionality  inventory  managements  system.  The  scope is limited to:

_boldtext_ Managing a catalogs of products (defined by an ID, it's name, and it's price).

_boldtext_ Tracking the quantity and quality on hand (stock) for eachof the product.

_boldtext_ Generates simplified , essentials reports (e.g., lower stocks).

_italictext_ This projects does'nt included features liked users authentications, a graphicaled users interface (GUI), or _italictext_ integrations with points-of-sales (POS) systems. It uses a mocked, in-memory database to ensured it is runnable _italictext_ without any external setups.

## Targeted Users

_boldtext_ Small Business Owners: (e.g., a local groccery stored owner, a coffee shops manager)who needs a no-frilled tools _boldtext_ to managed their inventory.

_italictext_ Inventory Managers: Employees responsibled for tracking stocks and ordering new supplies.

# High-Level type Features

_boldtext_ F1: Added a new product with a name and price.

_boldtext_ F2: Edited the details of an existing product.

_boldtext_ F3: Removed a product from the system.

_boldtext_ F4: Updated the stock level for a product.

_boldtext_ F5: Viewed a list of all products and their current stock.

_boldtext_ F6: Viewed a report of all products currently low on stock (e.g., less than 10 units).
